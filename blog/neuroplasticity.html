Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset

What is Neuroplasticity?

Initial thoughts:

Your brain is like any other muscle in your body, and can grow with use or shrink/deteriorate with disuse accordingly.

After looking around:

To me this definition was the clearest and most concise "the capacity for our brain cells to change in response to our behavior" So, pretty neat. And promising for the human existence.

Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people

It benefits them physically – by strengthening the same motor cortex (movement) neurons that doing the actual activity would. It also can be quite mentally freeing for people, because it can help them shed a sense of burden or lack of potential (feeling ‘not-smart’) and realise that change is always possible.

For those who are recovering from a medical event, it also brings a sense of hope – that their currently decreased faculties are not necessarily permanent, as significant positive changes can be achieved. This would also lead to increased feelings resilience – knowing that even if you hadn’t achieved something yet, it is still always a possibility by overcoming or engaging with effort and difficulty.

Discuss how you might engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for your own benefit

I’d like to mentally practice coding, as well as finding ways to retrain my brain out of anxious reactions.

What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity?

Learning or trying to do a new thing is the best way to increase neuroplasticity. The new thing needs to be:

  • Challenging/ a novelty – new to you and not too easy to learn (what)
  • An intention – have some meaning, importance or relevance to you so that you feel rewarded while doing it (why).
  • Specific – it will need focused or scheduled attention (how)
  • Repetitive and have intensity – do it for shorter bouts and often, and check in on progress with yourself (who do = you)
  • Focused longer term – be patient, as it takes time, and lapses happen (when). Refocusing is helpful and also compassion for yourself

What is 'Growth Mindset' and why is it relevant?

It’s the ability to believe that your intelligence is not fixed, and that it can be changed at any time. It's relevant, because we are on a learning journey here at EDA, and a growth mindset will help determine the outcome of and engagement with that journey.

In this exploration, did anything surprise you? Change for you?

I was surprised at how even just becoming aware of the growth mindset was enough to change outcomes. However, I had done some research during the prep work, so the surprise had also worn off a bit :) This didn’t change anything for me yet (yet intended!).

How will you integrate growth mindset into your learning journey?

In some ways I already have, but the mindfulness and refocusing will be key. I had always worked under the assumption that being harsh toward myself and always expecting more of myself was necessary to remain motivated, achieving and competitive. When I am late (which I frequently am to things – I can be a serial late-ist) I tend to apologize profusely, and then barrage myself with self-deprecation and criticism. This becomes a self-perpetuating cycle whereby I am so cruel to myself that I self-defeat, which was so frustrating because I would be aware I was doing it, but unable to stop myself. The part I was lacking was the self-compassion after the self-awareness. The way to integrate this into my learning journey is through daily mindfulness practices in the morning or evening, and frequent reflections/journalling. Additionally, self-compassion statements to acknowledge that lapses are normal and refocusing is achievable.
